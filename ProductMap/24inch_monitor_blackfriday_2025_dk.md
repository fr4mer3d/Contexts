## Important Shipping & Customs Information for Denmark

**Key Facts:**
- Standard VAT: 25% applied to all imported goods
- Goods under EUR 150: VAT only (no customs duty in most cases)
- Goods EUR 150+: Both VAT and customs duties apply
- Carrier (PostNord, GLS, DHL, TNT) typically handles VAT collection on delivery
- EU Customs ICS2 system (effective March 2025) applies to all shipments
- Estimated shipping from US/EU retailers: 7-21 business days to Denmark

**Recommendation:** Monitor total cost including VAT when calculating tier prices. Most cross-border retailers will estimate final cost including duty/VAT before checkout.

### Critical Mac Compatibility Issues & The 1440p vs 4K Debate

**This is the most debated topic across Mac communities:**

**Native 1440p Monitors - Community Consensus: AVOID for Mac**

Across MacRumors Forums and Apple Community discussions, the consensus is surprisingly strong and negative:
- 2560x1440 at native resolution on Mac creates "blurry and not sharp at all" text
- macOS rendering at non-integer scaling factors produces inferior visual quality compared to Windows
- Multiple community members report: "If the MacBook Pros are 10 out of 10, 1440p displays are 1 or 2 out of 10"
- The Dell P2423D overcomes this by being used at native resolution (not scaled), which works well

**4K at 24 Inches with 2x Scaling - Community Consensus: EXCELLENT for Mac**

This is where organic community wisdom diverges from marketing hype:
- 24" 4K (3840x2160) provides exact 2x pixel-perfect scaling on Mac ("looks like 1920x1080")
- ~185 PPI density described as "noticeable less resolution than M-series MacBook display but not objectionable"
- MacRumors technical community notes: "This is the sweet spot for 4K - not too large, pixel density excellent"
- Critical distinction: 24" 4K is fundamentally different from 27" 4K which creates scaling difficulties (too small native, too large at 2x)

**Community Street Wisdom:**
"27-inch 4K is in the bad zone (too small native, too big at 2x). 24-inch 4K is in the good zone (scales perfectly at 2x)." - MacRumors Forums consensus pattern

**Real-World Performance Concern:**
Some community members report that before macOS Ventura, users could manually choose scaling options on 4K monitors. After Ventura update, scaling choice disappeared, creating frustration for users who preferred non-2x scaling. However, M-series Macs specifically engineered to handle 4K scaling efficiently.
